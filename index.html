General winter sports safety tips

Don't let an accident on the first day ruin the whole week, remember a few simple tips and ensure a great time in the snow.

Wear a helmet

We know this seems like a really boring suggestion, but seriously - the amount of accidents and even deaths caused by head injuries have made many resorts set rules to make helmets mandatory when on the slopes.

Dress smart

First timers always seem to make the same mistake - you don't need to dress for the arctic! If you're skiing or snowboarding all day, you'll generally be keeping your body temperature quite high. Invest in a good coat and ski trousers. One will keep you warm, the other will keep your butt dry after you've landed on it for the hundredth time.

Don't go into the raped off areas

Again, this may seem an obvious one - but that fresh powder can look oh so appealing. Seriously though, there are so many horror stories about people getting lost or setting off avalanches - why take the risk?

Wear suntan lotion

Any seasoned pro knows that suntan lotion to the face is a must. Unless, ofcourse, you want to show off the skiers burn lines in the office the following week.

Know your limits

Winter sports - the clue is in the name. Most activities will use muscle groups that you probably haven't used in a while - or at all! Cramp is a common occurence and when you're halfway down a vertical slope, it can be pretty inconveninent! Ease yourself in - you've got a whole holiday ahead of you.
